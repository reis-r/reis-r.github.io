Fôrça Bruta!

저는 한국어를 조금 할 수 있어요!

Published: 23/12/2020
By Rony Reis
Category: misc
Tags: personal languages

Learning the basics of Esperanto when I was 15 opened my eyes to language learning. I grew up when video games were not translated to Portuguese (my native language), and having English everywhere around me, I managed to start catching meaning. I actually played with dictionaries as a boy, reading random pages and having joy when finding a weird word to play with. Learning a language is very rewarding, it allows you to get in touch with different cultures, textbooks to study another subjects, and just following the news… However, there is more to that: you learn new ways of thinking. I believe there is something in language learning that boosts the very core of our cognition.

Today I received the certificate for my first semester of Korean Language classes by the South Korean consulate. I am very grateful for this; it is a beautiful language, with an amazing writing system. The Korean people is blessed with good cuisine, entertainment and stunning landscapes. It is always nice to have a goal, so my aim (for now – of course) is to be able to follow the League Champions Korea (LCK) games.

The language is not as any other I have ever seen. Nevertheless, it is also the first language I learn with the help of a teacher from ground up. There are many challenges (I’m looking at you, ㄷ, ㄸ, and ㅌ), and I am far form proficient, but that is the start of an exciting journey. I started it with the right foot.
